# Contributing to the NWSS COE Resource Repository

This guide explains how COE leads and designated staff can submit resources for inclusion in the directory, update an existing entry, or report an issue. No prior GitHub experience is required.

---

## Who should submit

Submissions should come from **COE leads or designated staff** who are authorized to represent their COE and can verify that all submitted links are active. If you are unsure whether you are the appropriate submitter, confirm with your COE lead before proceeding.

---

## Submitting your COE's resources

### Step 1 — Open a new submission

Navigate to the **Issues** tab at the top of this repository and select **New Issue**. You will see a list of available templates. Choose **COE Resource Submission** and select **Get started**.

### Step 2 — Complete the form

The form is organized into seven sections. Work through each in sequence:

1. **COE Identification** — COE name, lead organization, point of contact, and jurisdictions served
2. **Web presence** — Primary website, resource hub or library page, and any additional URLs (dashboards, code repositories, shared drives). Include a brief description with each additional link.
3. **Resource categories** — Select all categories that reflect your COE's current offerings. An open-text field is provided for categories not listed.
4. **Access and format** — Whether resources are publicly available, restricted, or mixed, and in what formats they are provided. If access is restricted, note what is required to obtain it.
5. **Audience and pathogen scope** — Intended audiences and surveillance targets covered by your resources.
6. **Sharing and reuse** — Whether other COEs or jurisdictions may adapt your materials, and under what terms.
7. **Highlights and additional notes** — Optional but encouraged. Use this field to surface flagship resources, flag unique offerings, or provide context that would help others identify relevant materials quickly.

### Step 3 — Complete the submission checklist

Before submitting, confirm that all URLs are active, that you are authorized to submit on behalf of your COE, and that you understand a maintainer will review the submission before it is published.

### Step 4 — Submit

Select **Submit new issue**. Your submission will be automatically tagged `pending-review`. A maintainer will review within **5 business days** and may follow up through the issue thread before adding your entry to the index. You will receive a notification when your entry is live.

---

## Updating an existing entry

If your COE is already listed in the directory and you need to update information — new links, additional resource categories, a change in point of contact — open a new issue using the **COE Resource Submission** template. Note clearly at the top of the Highlights field that this is an update to an existing entry. The maintainer will replace the existing entry rather than create a duplicate.

---

## Reporting a broken link or requesting a correction

Open a new issue using the blank issue option and apply the label `correction`. Include the COE name, the affected URL or field, and the correct information. Corrections are typically resolved within 3 business days.

---

## Tips for a complete submission

- Verify that all URLs are accessible before submitting. Broken or login-walled links without access instructions will delay review.
- If resources are restricted, note in the Access Notes field what is required — account registration, COE affiliation, direct request, etc.
- The Highlights field is the most visible part of your directory entry for other COEs and federal partners. Use it to point to your most useful or most-used materials, not just a general description.
- If your COE's resources span multiple platforms or repositories, list each separately in the Additional Resource Links field with a brief description.
